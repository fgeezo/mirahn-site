Lockheed Martin - Cybersecurity & Software Engineer (College Student Tech)
During my time at Lockheed Martin, I worked as a cybersecurity/software engineer on their security-driven Mainframe Exit initiative. I originally joined for a 10-week internship, but the role continued into part-time work and eventually a Drexel co-op. I helped migrate employee data off an IBM mainframe by updating legacy JCL and COBOL pipelines and moving everything into AWS-based storage. Once these efforts were completed I acted as surge support assisting various teams that were contributing to a global onboarding portal. In doing so I had to assist in the development of the new employee experience while different privacy and security requirements across North America, the Middle East & North Africa, Latin America, and Europe.

Susquehanna Intl. Group - Blue Team Security Engineer Co-op
During my time at Susquehanna International Group, I worked as a Blue Team Security Engineer focusing on alert handling, detection engineering, and various security projects. I started development of a PowerShell module integrating ZeroFox APIs with our internal tooling to surface brand and VIP impersonations directly into our triage workflow. I also upgraded and reconfigured the firm’s Windows Event Collector servers, modernizing the legacy hosts thereby improving Sysmon/workstation log ingestion across 1,000+ endpoints. Day-to-day, I investigated employee-reported malicious emails and wrote custom regular expressions to block malspam campaigns. I handled a high volume of SIEM alerts using Sysmon/WEC, MDE, and network telemetry, documenting all findings. I contributed to detection engineering by tuning and creating rules, validating existing content, writing playbooks, and adding IOCs during investigations. I also spent two weeks with the security assurance team, helping craft phishing simulation emails based on real cases I handled and assisting with policy exception and TPSA workflows.
